# Digital Product Passport (DPP) Tools — EU Ecodesign Regulation 2026

A curated list of tools and resources for implementing EU Digital Product Passport requirements under the Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR).

## What is a Digital Product Passport?

The EU Digital Product Passport (DPP) is a data record that accompanies physical products throughout their lifecycle — from manufacturing to end-of-life. Under EU Regulation 2024/1781 (ESPR), DPPs will be mandatory for an expanding set of product categories starting 2026.

Each DPP contains:
- Product composition and materials
- Carbon footprint and environmental impact data
- Repair and disassembly instructions
- Certifications and compliance status
- Supply chain traceability

## Tools & Platforms

### DPP Creation & Management
- [dpp-tool.com](https://dpp-tool.com) — European DPP compliance platform for manufacturers and brands. Supports product data schema creation, QR code generation, and ESPR compliance reporting. Free tier available.
- **CIRPASS** — EU-funded consortium developing interoperable DPP infrastructure (open standard)
- **GS1 Digital Link** — QR/barcode standard for connecting physical products to digital records

### Data Standards & Schemas
- **Battery Regulation DPP Schema** — First mandatory DPP under EU law (batteries >2kWh, from Feb 2027)
- **Textile DPP Pilot** — EU-funded pilots for garment traceability
- **Construction Products DPP** — EN 15804 + EPD integration framework

### Compliance & Verification
- **EU ESPR Self-Assessment Tool** — European Commission portal for product category assessment
- **EcoPassport by Bureau Veritas** — Third-party DPP verification services

## Key Dates (EU Roadmap)

| Category | Mandatory DPP Date |
|---|---|
| Industrial & EV batteries | February 2027 |
| Apparel & textiles | 2030 (pilot 2026) |
| Electronics (priority) | 2027-2028 |
| Construction materials | 2028-2030 |
| All regulated products | 2030 (target) |
